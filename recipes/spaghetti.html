Ingredients

  • Pasta (spaghetti)
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ground beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with water and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add a generous amount of salt to the boiling water.
  3. Add the spaghetti pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  4. While the pasta is cooking, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  5. Add minced garlic and chopped onion to the pan and sauté until they become fragrant and translucent.
  6. Add ground beef to the pan and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spatula.
  7. Drain excess fat from the pan if necessary.
  8. Pour tomato sauce into the pan with the cooked ground beef, garlic, and onion.
  9. Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, dried oregano, dried basil, and red pepper flakes (if desired).
  10. Simmer the sauce for about 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  11. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it in a colander.
  12. Return the drained pasta to the pot and add a small amount of olive oil to prevent sticking.
  13. Pour the sauce over the cooked pasta and toss until the pasta is evenly coated.
  14. Serve the spaghetti hot, and sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top.
